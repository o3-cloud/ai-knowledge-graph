## Overview

Owen Zanzal introduces AI Katasâ€”a comprehensive collection of 100 structured exercises designed to develop proficiency in AI prompting and system design through deliberate, progressive practice. Rather than relying on passive learning or sporadic experimentation, AI Katas provide a systematic pathway for building AI literacy across ten core competency areas, from foundational prompt engineering to sophisticated multi-agent and multi-modal system architecture.

The framework acknowledges that AI capabilities are evolving rapidly, and practitioners need structured methods to develop competency systematically. Each kata functions as a focused practice exercise with clear success criteria, progressive difficulty levels, and explicit skill targets.

## The Need for Structured AI Practice

### Why AI Katas Matter

**The challenge:**
AI capabilities are advancing faster than our ability to build reliable mental models. Engineers, designers, and product managers need practical frameworks to develop competency at pace with technology evolution.

**Traditional learning gaps:**
- Ad hoc experimentation lacks systematic progression
- Online courses often feel theoretical or outdated
- Hands-on practice lacks clear success criteria
- No standardized progression path from novice to expert
- Individual practitioners work in isolation

**The kata approach:**
Borrowing from martial arts and programming traditions, structured exercises that isolate specific skills, provide clear objectives, and enable measurable progression.

### Why This Matters Now

**Rapid capability evolution:**
- New model releases monthly with different strengths
- Techniques from six months ago often obsolete
- Organizations need rapid upskilling
- Individual contributors need independent learning paths

**Business impact:**
Teams with AI fluency ship faster, make better architectural decisions, and recover faster from failures.

**Deliberate practice at scale:**

**Principles:**
1. **Small units** â€” Each kata focuses on one skill

## How to Use AI Katas

### Implementation Approaches

**1. Sequential Progression**
Start with Set 1, progress through all 100 in order.

**Use when:**
- Building complete AI literacy from scratch
- Team training program
- Individual comprehensive development

**Timeline:** 3-6 months (regular practice)

**2. Targeted Skill Training**
Select specific katas matching current gaps.

**Use when:**
- Addressing specific weaknesses
- Preparing for particular projects
- Focused skill improvement

**Timeline:** 1-4 weeks per skill

**3. Daily Kata Practice**
One kata daily, rotating through sets.

**Use when:**
- Maintaining and improving fluency
- Regular skills development
- Individual practice routine

**Timeline:** Ongoing, 15-30 minutes/day

**4. Team Competitions**
Groups solve same kata, compare approaches.

**Use when:**
- Cross-team skill building
- Benchmarking capabilities
- Learning from peer approaches

**Timeline:** Weekly or monthly

**5. Curriculum Design**
Build custom learning paths for specific roles.

**Use when:**
- Organization-specific requirements
- Different role needs (PM vs. Engineer vs. Designer)
- Layered skill acquisition

**Timeline:** Custom per role

## Using AI Katas in Practice

### Practice Mode

**Typical workflow:**
1. Select a kata matching current skill level or gap
2. Read the objective clearly
3. Craft your prompt or system design
4. Test against the kata criteria
5. Iterate until success
6. Analyze your approach

**What you're building:**
- Intuition about what works
- Mental models of AI behavior
- Pattern recognition across domains
- Judgment about tradeoffs

### Evaluation Mode

**Assessment approach:**
- Run same kata against multiple models (GPT-4, Claude, Gemini, etc.)
- Compare results; understand model differences
- Identify which prompts are model-dependent
- Build mental models of model strengths/weaknesses

**Value:**
Deep understanding of what makes systems reliable vs. fragile.

### Team Training

**Structured approach:**
1. Assign kata set to team
2. Have team members complete individually
3. Share approaches; discuss differences
4. Establish team preferences and patterns
5. Develop shared vocabulary and mental models

**Timeline:** 1-2 weeks per set

### Curriculum Building

**Creating custom paths:**
1. Identify required competencies for role
2. Select relevant katas from framework
3. Order by dependency and difficulty
4. Measure progress through completion
5. Adapt based on performance

### Agent Development Training

**Building agent team expertise:**
- Use advanced katas (60+) to develop sophisticated understanding
- Practice multi-agent orchestration patterns
- Understand failure modes
- Design recovery strategies
- Build mental models of agent limitations
